About Me

As a Web Platform Engineer at ResMed Inc. , I specialize in network programming, cloud technologies, DevOps practices, and ensuring site reliability. My journey began as an intern at Maxis, where I collaborated closely with the Infrastructure and Network Automation Department.

Before stepping into my current role, I pursued my undergraduate studies at the Faculty of Computer Science and Information Technology (FCSIT), University of Malaya, Malaysia. Under the guidance of Prof. Teck Chaw Ling, I undertook a senior-year thesis focusing on the area of Software-Defined Networking (SDN).

My Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, with a specialization in Computer Systems & Networks, was completed with distinction, achieving a CGPA of 3.90 out of 4.00.

Research Interest

I'm deeply interested in the domain of programmable networking, data-driven networking and traffic engineering in Software-Defined Networks (SDN) . My interest has led me to engage with internationally funded SDN projects such as the Open/Federated Playground for Future Networks (OF@TEIN+++), where I had the privilege of serving as a Student Fellow.

Within this framework, my focus revolved around data plane programming with P4 and creating OpenFlow controller plane applications aimed at optimizing traffic rerouting through the utilization of performance metrics. This involved crafting a programmable traffic generation bot utilizing tools like Scapy, Iperf, and DITG to simulate diverse traffic types, including TCP and UDP. Moreover, I delved into the realm of real-life traffic scenarios by leveraging datasets containing labeled network traffic flows, thereby mimicking authentic network conditions.
